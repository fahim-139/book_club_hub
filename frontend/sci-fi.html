Science Fiction Books

Journey through futuristic landscapes and technological wonders:

  • Dune

    by Frank Herbert

    Set on the desert planet Arrakis, "Dune" explores politics, religion, and ecology as Paul Atreides becomes the mysterious Muad'Dib and leads a revolution.

  • Neuromancer

    by William Gibson

    A seminal cyberpunk novel following a washed-up computer hacker hired for a final job in a dystopian future dominated by multinational corporations.

  • The Left Hand of Darkness

    by Ursula K. Le Guin

    On the icy planet Gethen, a lone ambassador faces political intrigue and explores themes of gender and identity in this groundbreaking sci-fi classic.

  • Snow Crash

    by Neal Stephenson

    A fast-paced cyberpunk adventure combining ancient mythology and futuristic tech as Hiro Protagonist battles a virus threatening both the real and virtual worlds.

  • The Three-Body Problem

    by Liu Cixin

    A Chinese sci-fi masterpiece where scientists make first contact with an alien civilization amid political and scientific upheaval.

  • Foundation

    by Isaac Asimov

    A visionary saga of a declining galactic empire and the psychohistorian trying to save civilization through mathematical prediction.

  • Ender's Game

    by Orson Scott Card

    Young Ender Wiggin trains at a military school in space to defend Earth from an alien threat in this compelling and thoughtful military sci-fi.

  • The Martian

    by Andy Weir

    Stranded on Mars, astronaut Mark Watney must use his ingenuity and humor to survive and signal for rescue in this thrilling survival story.

  • Brave New World

    by Aldous Huxley

    A chilling dystopian vision where humans are genetically engineered and controlled through conditioning and drugs in a futuristic society.

  • Hyperion

    by Dan Simmons

    A complex and epic tale featuring multiple travelers recounting stories as they journey to a distant planet under threat in a richly imagined universe.
